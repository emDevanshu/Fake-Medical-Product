About Our Project

The manufacturing and marketing of counterfeit or duplicate products and goods lead to consequential financial, health, and safety threats to end users. It also impacts the economic growth of original manufacturers and businesses through revenue loss, product defamation, downtime, replacement expenses, forcing brands to spend money fighting counterfeits, putting trust among business partners at risk, and stealing sales.

To overcome these crucial effects of counterfeiting, a Blockchain-based system is used for identifying original products. While existing methods like RFID tags, Artificial Intelligence, and QR-based systems have their drawbacks, this project integrates the strengths of each.

In this approach, emerging technologies like wireless networks, QR codes, and Blockchain provide a robust defense against counterfeit production. Products are scanned using a camera-based QR system — each QR is linked to the Blockchain, storing verified product details and unique identifiers as immutable blocks.

If the QR code matches, a notification confirming authenticity is sent to the customer. If it doesn’t, both the customer and manufacturer are notified of the counterfeit attempt. This ensures that consumers can independently verify product authenticity without relying solely on merchants.
